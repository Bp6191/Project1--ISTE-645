Our Fact Checks on Things President-Elect Trump Allegedly Said During 2024 Campaign

Trump made many claims while on the campaign trail.

By Izz Scott LaMagdeleine Published Nov. 9, 2024

U.S. President-elect Donald Trump made many claims on the campaign trail while seeking the nation's highest office again. We looked into many of them, like whether Trump took credit for overturning Roe v. Wade (true) or if whether said he plans to cut Medicare and Social Security if reelected (mixture).

Some of the claims Trump made that we've fact-checked involved his Democratic opponent, Vice President Kamala Harris. One such claim was that Harris had backed out of a Sept. 4 Fox News debate with Trump (false), while another was that Harris "doesn't want anybody saying Merry Christmas" (false).

In the run-up to the 2024 election, we fact-checked other claims surrounding Trump and Harris, as well as claims about their running mates, Ohio Sen. JD Vance and Minnesota Gov. Tim Walz.

Even Trump's son Donald Trump Jr. shared the headline and claimed, "It took about 12 hours after my father's election win for Hamas to call for peace! @realDonaldTrump isn't even president yet and he's already getting it done. Spectacular."
